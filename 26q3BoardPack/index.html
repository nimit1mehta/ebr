Methodology
  • GRR (P): risk-weighted company renewed - resolved accounts at actual, in-flight accounts at 2025 ARR x retention(risk) [Low 100%, Medium 90%, High 75%], capped at prior; plus multi-year locked at 100% + partner at 100%, over the 2025 company base.   • 2026 ARR (P) — ties exactly to: Gross renewed (Renewal book x GRR(P): resolved actuals + in-flight risk-weighted) $3.37M + At-renewal uplift (organic only; conversion-at-renewal growth is booked as upsell) $178K + Multi-year renewals (locked, retained at 100% = prior) $1.63M + Bookings New+Upsell+Expansion (closed actuals + weighted open pipeline) $1.62M + Partner ARR @100% $750K = $7.54M. Methodology and sources
Projection
  • Retention by risk (open accounts): Low 100%, Medium 90%, High 75%, applied to the account's 2025 ARR. Risk is the renewal Risk level.
  • Projected 2026 ARR: resolved accounts use the Customer Reporting actual; open accounts use 2025 ARR × retention(risk). This changes PROJECTED ARR only - ACTUAL and RETAINED are unchanged.
Formulas
  • GRR (P) [company]: (book gross retained + multi-year-locked 2025 ARR + partner ARR, all retained, capped at prior, no uplift) divided by the company 2025 software base (book + locked + partner). This is the company % gross renewed - the headline GRR.
  • % Gross Renewed (book): book gross retained divided by the book 2025 ARR - a book-subset retention rate, not the company GRR.
  • NRR (P): (sum of 2026 actual-or-projected over the book + multi-year-locked 2026 ARR + partner ARR + bookings upsell & expansion) divided by the 2025 base (book 2025 ARR + locked 2025 ARR + partner). NRR captures uplift/upsell; GRR does not.
Sources
  • Customer Reporting "2026 Renewals" tab (canonical), budget software register, retention rates by risk (this section). Multi-year locked: register multi_year_through > 2026.
